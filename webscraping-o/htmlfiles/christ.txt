      <h1 style="color:#ff0000; text-align:center;">
       Merry Christmas!
      </h1>
      <div style="font-size:small;">
       12/24/20
      </div>
      <br/>
      <br/>
      It has been a
      <b>
       <i>
        LONG
       </i>
      </b>
      year, but we finally made it through 2020, and we are ready to celebrate Christmas! After a year of pandemic, riots, elections, political division, growing hatred, and layoffs... (long list), it is time to remember that God is good, all the time, and that our problems reflect the fact that we live in a fallen world. So as we celebrate the birth, death, and resurrection of God's Son Jesus Christ, it is also time to help the people that have come upon hard times.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      To deal with the extended suffering this year, we would ask that everybody increase their level of giving to local food banks, homeless shelters, medical charities, and of course your local Church, along with other places that are helping people get through hard times. Most major cities have homeless shelters and food banks that are both offering help to those in need, and asking for donations to support their mission. If you are capable of helping at all, then please use a search engine or other means to find charitable organizations in your neighborhood, for example, search for the phrase "
      <b>
       <u>
        Food Shelters Near Me
       </u>
      </b>
      ", or "
      <b>
       <u>
        Homeless Shelters Near Me
       </u>
      </b>
      " (assuming you have browser location turned on). If you don't already know the charity (and link URL), then check out whether the charity is trustworthy before donating... (stewardship)
      <br/>
      <br/>
      <hr style="width:30%;"/>
      <br/>
      <br/>
      Here are a few national and global charities that need your support, in addition to the donations needed by local food banks, shelters, and charities. Of course the Salvation Army has been helping people for over 150 years, and has programs in most big cities. Samaritan's Purse has been helping people worldwide for 50 years, and even responded with tents and mobile medical units during the early stages of the pandemic. I also regularly recommend Wounded Warriors Project as a great charitable organization that supports the people that protect the freedoms and rights we all love as a nation.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      These are just a few suggestions for giving during the Christmas season (and every season).

      <h3 style="text-align:center;">
       The China Supply Chain
      </h3>
      The recent threats from China to terminate shipments of critical drug making supplies should be a wake-up call... to reduce our reliance on unreliable countries like China (especially for critical products and services). The threats from China are for things that were originally Made in the USA (until we completely offshored that capability to China).

      <br/>
      <br/>
      <h3 style="color: red; text-align:center;">
       Happy New Year too!
      </h3>
      We have been running this site for almost 24 years, and Lord willing we will run it for years to come (or until He comes, or comes for me!) Along the way we have been through a number of challenges, and have broken our fair share of links and other parts of the site. This last year (ok, year++) has been a hard year. There were big changes in the home life as we moved our home to Idaho, and during that move we certainly broke "more than our share" of things.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      In the last few years there has been considerable controversy over the tracking of personal information on the net. For many years we allowed users to contact each other in the prayer section of the site, using encoded contact information. In the past, we allowed users (it was never required) to enter an email address for response to their prayer post.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      Given the controversy over tracking of personal information, we decided to totally remove contact information from the prayer posts (and from the server). We would love to hear from people in the prayer/praise section, but no longer ask for email information (as a precaution). The Lord always knows the heart of the posters. We will continue to use cookies for site operations, but users are always able to disable cookies, and no personal information is stored in the cookies.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      We have also moved parts of the site to a cloud-based version (Cloudflare), which should allow us to reach parts of the world that are known for electronic attacks over the net (hacking, cracking the server, denial of service attacks, etc..., we get "more than our share" of attacks). We hope to add additional content to the cloud-based version of the site this year, and keep it up to date.
      <br/>

      Have a very Merry Christmas,
