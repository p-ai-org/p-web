  <div class="body">
   <h1>
    Pocoo
   </h1>
   <p>
    Pocoo was a loosely assembled team of Open Source developers working on some very popular
    Python projects.  Out of this came the
    <a href="http://pygments.org/">
     Pygments
    </a>
    syntax
    highlighter, the
    <a href="http://www.sphinx-doc.org/">
     Sphinx
    </a>
    documentation system,
    the
    <a href="https://www.palletsprojects.com/p/flask/">
     Flask
    </a>
    web framework, the
    <a href="https://www.palletsprojects.com/p/jinja/">
     Jinja
    </a>
    templating engine, the
    <a href="https://www.palletsprojects.com/p/werkzeug/">
     Werkzeug
    </a>
    WSGI library, the
    <a href="https://www.palletsprojects.com/p/click/">
     Click
    </a>
    command line toolkit and
    much more.  It was led by
    <a href="https://github.com/mitsuhiko">
     Armin Ronacher
    </a>
    and
    <a href="https://github.com/birkenfeld">
     Georg Brandl
    </a>
    .
   </p>
   <p>
    As projects get more mature more structure is needed.  This means that the Pocoo projects
    now have new places and teams that take care of them:
   </p>
   <ul>
    <li>
     <a href="https://www.palletsprojects.com/">
      The Pallets Projects
     </a>
     is the new home
      and team for Flask, Werkzeug, ItsDangerous, Jinja, MarkupSafe, Werkzeug and Click.
    </li>
    <li>
     <a href="http://www.sphinx-doc.org/">
      sphinx-doc.org
     </a>
     is the new home of the
      Sphinx documentation tool.
    </li>
    <li>
     <a href="http://pygments.org/">
      pygments.org
     </a>
     is the new home of the pygments
      project.
    </li>
   </ul>
   <p>
    Armin's blog stays at
    <a href="http://lucumr.pocoo.org/">
     lucumr.pocoo.org
    </a>
    .
   </p>
