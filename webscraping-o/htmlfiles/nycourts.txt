          <p><strong>Mental Hygiene Law Hearings Conducted Remotely<br />
            Tuesday, March 17th, 2020<br />
            Supreme Court, New York County, 60 Centre Street, NY City</strong></p>
          <p>On Tuesday, March 17th, 2020, four Mental Hygiene Law hearings were conducted with individuals currently receiving care at Bellevue Hospital Center and the VA Medical Center. The Judge, clerk, court reporter and interpreter, if needed, are all at 60 Centre Street. Attorneys, clients, doctors and other witnesses joined from various locations using videoconferencing technology. 
            
            The Court, partnering with our Mental Hygiene working group of professionals, has been able to go forward with these critically important hearings without any delay. </p>

          <p>On  Monday, March 17th, 2020, the New York State Court of Appeals, the  State&rsquo;s highest Court began its March 2020 session at Court of Appeals Hall in  Albany. The Court remains open to the public, subject to the precautions  recommended by State and national health authorities. Counsel who are scheduled  to argue but are unable to appear in person by reason of the current public  health emergency may contact the Clerk&rsquo;s Office and request other arrangements,  including oral argument by videoconference. </p>

	    <p><strong>Video Arraignments Start in New York City<br />
	      Tuesday, March 17th, 2020
	      <br />
	      New York City Criminal Court, 100 Centre Street</strong><br />
	      <br />
	      On Tuesday, March 17th, 2020, videoconference arraignments began in New York City Criminal Court. All parties but the defendant appeared in the courtroom. Healthy or non-COVID-19-involved defendants will be produced at the respective County’s central booking. There will be two video feeds – one for attorney-defendant interview and one for court appearance.	    </p>
	    <p>Judge Kevin B. McGrath, Supervising Judge, New York County Criminal Court, conducted one of the first arraignments.	      </p>
	    <p>An increasing number of Coronavirus cases has led to minimizing contact between judicial and non-judicial staff and the public. </p>

Please be assured that the New York State Court System is monitoring developments  closely and is working with our partners at all government levels to take  appropriate measures as the situation progresses, and to ensure that  courthouses statewide are prioritizing the health and safety of all visitors.  Please continue to visit this site for updates on the latest developments  concerning the New York State Courts. </p>
