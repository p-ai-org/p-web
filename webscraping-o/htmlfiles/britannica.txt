           <h3>
            World Poetry Day
           </h3>
           <div class="font-serif mt-20">
            On March 21 we celebrate poets and their work. To honour the day, we’ve listed some of the best-known poems of the last 100 years or so. Can you name the author?
           </div>
           <a class="font-weight-bold mt-10 mb-5 d-inline-block" href="/biography/Robert-Frost?utm_medium=mendel-homepage&amp;utm_source=oyr&amp;utm_campaign=oyr-1&amp;utm_term=20210321">
            “The Road Not Taken”
           </a>
           <div class="font-serif">
            This widely beloved 1915 poem was actually written as a joke to a friend.
           </div>
           <a class="font-weight-bold mt-10 mb-5 d-inline-block" href="/biography/Sylvia-Plath?utm_medium=mendel-homepage&amp;utm_source=oyr&amp;utm_campaign=oyr-2&amp;utm_term=20210321">
            “Daddy”
           </a>
           <div class="font-serif">
            An exploration of a woman’s conflicting feelings over her father’s death, this work appeared in the collection
            <em>
             Ariel
            </em>
            (1965), which was published two years after its author’s death.
           </div>
           <a class="font-weight-bold mt-10 mb-5 d-inline-block" href="/biography/Maya-Angelou?utm_medium=mendel-homepage&amp;utm_source=oyr&amp;utm_campaign=oyr-3&amp;utm_term=20210321">
            “Still I Rise”
           </a>
           <div class="font-serif">
            Written in 1978, this poem continues to resonate with its exploration of the African American experience.
           </div>
           <a class="font-weight-bold mt-10 mb-5 d-inline-block" href="/biography/Allen-Ginsberg?utm_medium=mendel-homepage&amp;utm_source=oyr&amp;utm_campaign=oyr-4&amp;utm_term=20210321">
            “Howl”
           </a>
           <div class="font-serif">
            A denunciation of the weaknesses and failings of American society, this 1956 work is perhaps the most significant product of the Beat movement.
           </div>
           <a class="font-weight-bold mt-10 mb-5 d-inline-block" href="/biography/E-E-Cummings?utm_medium=mendel-homepage&amp;utm_source=oyr&amp;utm_campaign=oyr-5&amp;utm_term=20210321">
            “[i carry your heart with me(I carry it in)]”
           </a>
           <div class="font-serif">
            This poem become a staple at weddings following its publication in 1952.

           <h2 class="mb-20">
            First Day of Spring
           </h2>
           <div class="font-serif mb-30">
            Toss that hot beverage, pack up your sweaters, and bring out the sandals. Spring officially starts on March 20 with the arrival of the vernal equinox in the Northern Hemisphere. This means longer days, warmer weather, growing plants, and mating animals.

          Politics, Law &amp; Government
         </a>
         <div class="font-serif mt-10 line-clamp clamp-3">
          The world today is divided territorially into more than 190 countries, each of which possesses a national government that claims to exercise sovereignty and seeks to compel obedience to its will by its citizens. Governments can be classified in any number of ways. For example, they might be classified by the number of rulers, thus distinguishing government by one (as in a monarchy or a tyranny) from government by the few (in an aristocracy or oligarchy) and from government by the many (as in a democracy). Governments can also be classified by mode of succession; for example, ascension to governmental leadership may follow the rules of hereditary succession, or it may be determined through elections or by force. Governments also vary in terms of the laws and rules of conduct that each political entity follows.
