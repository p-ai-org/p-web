      <h3>
       What is Do Not Track?
      </h3>
      <p>
       DNT is a web browser setting that requests that a web application disable its tracking of an individual user. When you choose to turn on the DNT setting in your browser, your browser sends a special signal to websites, analytics companies, ad networks, plug in providers, and other web services you encounter while browsing to stop tracking your activity.
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="medium-3 columns on-off">
      <img alt="DNT Indicator" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/themes/allaboutdnt/assets/images/dnt-off.svg"/>
      <h5>
       You are being tracked
      </h5>
      <p>
       Your browser is telling sites that you affirmatively want to be tracked.
      </p>

       Adjust Settings

         <h2>
          The Future of DNT
         </h2>
         <p>
          <span id="u113-26">
           Although there is no universally-agreed upon standard for what a company should do when it detects a DNT signal, an international working group of policy advocates, technical experts, and companies is attempting to create a consensus interpretation.  For more information about this effort,
          </span>
          <span id="u113-27">
          </span>
          <a class="nonblock" href="https://www.w3.org/2011/tracking-protection/drafts/dnt-for-users.html" target="_blank">
           visit the W3C Tracking Protection Working Group
          </a>
          <span id="u113-30">
           .
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Additionally, new technologies now allow venues such as airports, stores, and hotels to receive signals from devices that are in or near them. Mobile location analytics companies that have agreed to FPF’s
          <a href="http://www.futureofprivacy.org/wp-content/uploads/10.22.13-FINAL-MLA-Code.pdf" target="blank">
           Mobile Location Analytics Code of Conduct
          </a>
          will honor the requests of consumers who wish to opt-out of having their location collected. Consumers can opt-out by entering their phones’ Wi-Fi or Bluetooth MAC address at
          <a href="http://www.smartstoreprivacy.org/" target="blank">
           smartstoreprivacy.org.
          </a>
         </p>

       <div class="medium-6 columns">
        <a data-open="mobileModal">

        <div class="small reveal" data-reveal="" id="mobileModal">
         <h2>
          Adjust Settings for Mobile Devices
         </h2>
         <p>
          Leading mobile internet browsers support DNT in various ways. Click on your mobile browser to reveal how to adjust its DNT feature.
         </p>
         <ul class="accordion" data-accordion="" data-allow-all-closed="true">
          <li class="accordion-item is-active" data-accordion-item="">
           <a class="accordion-title">
            <img class="settings-icon" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/opera-150x150.png"/>
            Opera
           </a>
           <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content="">
            <p>
             Opera’s Mini Browser does not currently have a Do Not Track feature available.
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="accordion-item" data-accordion-item="">
           <a class="accordion-title">
            <img class="settings-icon" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/firefox-150x150.png"/>
            Firefox
           </a>
           <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content="">
            <p>
             Firefox’s Do-not-track feature is turned off by default.  To turn it on, scroll and find the
             <strong>
              Settings
             </strong>
             application.  Find and tap the
             <strong>
              Do Not Track
             </strong>
             item, under the
             <strong>
              Privacy &amp; Security
             </strong>
             category.  Tap the circle right of the Do Not Track label to enable or disable this feature.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="firefox os" class="alignnone size-full" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/firefox-os.jpg"/>
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="accordion-item" data-accordion-item="">
           <a class="accordion-title">
            <img class="settings-icon" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/ie-150x150.png"/>
            Internet Explorer 10
           </a>
           <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content="">
            <ul>
             <li>
              <strong>
               For Windows Phone 8:
              </strong>
              In the App list, tap
              <strong>
               Settings, flick left to Applications, tap Internet Explorer,
              </strong>
              and then tap
              <strong>
               Advanced Settings.
              </strong>
              Then select or clear the
              <strong>
               “Send a Do Not Track request to websites you visit”
              </strong>
              check box to enable or disable this feature.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               For Windows Phone 7:
              </strong>
              In Internet Explorer, tap
              <strong>
               More &gt; Settings.
              </strong>
              (You can also access Internet Explorer’s settings through Settings on the App list.) From there you can select or deselect
              <strong>
               “Allow Internet Explorer to collect my browsing history.”
              </strong>
             </li>
            </ul>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="accordion-item" data-accordion-item="">
           <a class="accordion-title">
            <img class="settings-icon" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/chrome-150x150.png"/>
            Chrome
           </a>
           <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content="">
            <p>
             To turn on or off DNT in Chrome, go to
             <strong>
              Chrome menu &gt; Settings &gt; (Advanced) Privacy,
             </strong>
             then select your preferred option for “Do Not Track.”
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="chrome os dnt" class="alignnone size-full" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/chrome-os-dnt.jpg"/>
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="accordion-item" data-accordion-item="">
           <a class="accordion-title">
            <img class="settings-icon" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/safari-150x150.png"/>
            Safari
           </a>
           <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content="">
            <p>
             There are two ways to turn on Do Not Track on iOS.  On iOS 6 or 7, you can adjust Safari’s Do Not Track setting is in Settings &gt; Safari &gt; “Private Browsing.” (On iOS 7, the setting is simply labeled “Do Not Track.”) Enabling private browsing will also turn on Do Not Track.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="ipad private browsing" class="alignnone size-full" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/ipad-private-browsing.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              iPad
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="private browsing image" class="alignnone size-full" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/private-browsing-image.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              iPhone running iOS 6
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="dnt ios7" class="alignnone size-full" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/dnt-ios7.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              iPhone running iOS 7
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             In addition, you can also enable Do Not Track on iOS 7 through the Safari Browser itself: to do this, open Safari and tap the tabs icon on the lower right hand corner. Then tap the lower left hand corner marked “Private” to enable Private Browsing for that session. Doing so also enables Do Not Track.
             <br/>
             <img alt="dnt ios7 safari" class="alignnone size-full" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/dnt-ios7-safari.jpg"/>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              iPhone running iOS
             </strong>
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          In addition to browser controls, many phones have a separate DNT setting for mobile apps. Note that setting your browser to DNT does not necessarily affect your DNT setting for apps. To make sure you are not tracked, you must turn on BOTH of these options.
         </p>
         <ul class="accordion" data-accordion="" data-allow-all-closed="true">
          <li class="accordion-item" data-accordion-item="">
           <a class="accordion-title">
            <img class="settings-icon" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/apple-flat-150x150.png"/>
            Apple iOS
           </a>
           <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content="">
            <p>
             For iPhones/iPads running iOS 6, you can Limit Ad Tracking for apps by navigating to
             <strong>
              Settings &gt; General &gt; About &gt; Advertising &gt; Limit Ad Tracking
             </strong>
             and turning the feature on.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="limit ad tracking" class="alignnone size-full" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/limit-ad-tracking.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             On iOS 7, the option is now also available at
             <strong>
              Settings &gt; Privacy &gt; Advertising.
             </strong>
             You can also manage the privacy settings for individual apps on iOS 6 or 7 by navigating to
             <strong>
              Settings &gt; Privacy
             </strong>
             and selecting the type of data you would like to manage app settings for.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="limit ad tracking os7" class="alignnone size-full" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/limit-ad-tracking-os7.jpg"/>
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="accordion-item" data-accordion-item="">
           <a class="accordion-title">
            <img class="settings-icon" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/android-flat-150x150.png"/>
            Android
           </a>
           <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content="">
            <p>
             Google’s new Advertising ID for apps is available for devices running Android 2.3 or later. In Google Settings,  select “Ads”  and you will see your Advertising ID, which is used to deliver targeted ads on apps. On this screen you can select the option to reset your ad ID, which functions essentially like clearing your cookies in a browser. Additionally, tap the box labeled “Opt Out of interest-based ads”  to opt out.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="google ads" class="alignnone size-full" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/google-ads.jpg"/>
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="accordion-item" data-accordion-item="">
           <a class="accordion-title">
            <img class="settings-icon" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/windows-8-150x150.png"/>
            Windows OS
           </a>
           <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content="">
            <p>
             Windows does not currently offer granular privacy settings for apps.
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="accordion-item" data-accordion-item="">
           <a class="accordion-title">
            <img class="settings-icon" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/firefox-150x150.png"/>
            Firefox OS
           </a>
           <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content="">
            <p>
             The Do-not-track feature is turned off by default.  To turn it on, scroll and find the Settings application.  Find and tap the Do Not Track item, under the Privacy &amp; Security category.  Tap the circle right of the Do Not Track label to enable or disable this feature.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="firefox os" class="alignnone size-full" src="//allaboutdnt.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/firefox-os-1.jpg"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             Note that enabling DNT on the Firefox OS will send a signal to both the Firefox browser as well as individual apps that you do not wish to be tracked.
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
         </ul>

      <div class="reveal" data-reveal="" id="companiesTrackingModal">
       <h2>
        Why Do Companies Track You?
       </h2>
       <p>
        Web services track users for a wide variety of reasons.  Web sites and analytics companies may track to know how many unique users visit their sites, or to learn how users navigate within a site.  Advertising networks may track you in order to measure the effectiveness of the ads they serve, or to create a web surfing profile for you that is used to create more personalized ads.  A mobile advertiser promoting an app may track you on your mobile devices in order to understand which of their ad campaigns resulted in more downloads of their app.
       </p>
       <p>
        Tracking is also used to prevent fraud. For example, an online bank might use tracking techniques to detect that a certain web browser is trying to log in to an account from an unfamiliar location.   An ad network that gets paid by the number of users that click might also track in order to recognize fake clicks generated by computer bots.
       </p>
       <p>
        There is a wide range of views on the benefits and privacy implications of tracking. You can find industry views at
        <a href="http://www.youradchoices.com/" target="_blank">
         <span id="u1532-10">
          Ad Choices
         </span>
        </a>

        </span>
        <span id="u1532-14">

         .

          donottrack.us
         </span>
        </a>
        <span id="u1532-20">
         .
        </span>
        <span id="u1532-20">
         For the views of Future of Privacy forum leaders, visit
