       <div class="col-12">
        <h3 class="mb-2" id="how_to_tag_your_javascript_scripts">
         How to tag your JavaScript scripts
        </h3>
        <p>
         Tag any JavaScript
         <code>
          &lt;script&gt;
         </code>
         that you're using by changing
         <code>
          type="text/javascript"
         </code>
         to
         <code>
          type="text/plain"
         </code>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Then add the corresponding consent level for each tagged
         <code>
          &lt;script&gt;
         </code>
         . For example:
         <code>
          cookie-consent="functionality"
         </code>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          That's it.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Here's an example from Google Analytics and a Login Session script that's loaded with some necessary cookies:
        </p>
        <br/>
        <pre>&lt;!-- Login Cookies --&gt;

      <br/>
      <div class="row">
       <div class="col-12">
        <div class="form-group">
         <h2 class="h4 mb-4">
          What is Cookie Consent?
         </h2>
         <p class="mb-2">
          <strong>
           Cookie Consent
          </strong>
          (cookieconsent.com) is a free cookies management solution that you can integrate on your website that helps you comply with the EU Cookies Directive and GDPR.
         </p>
         <p class="mb-5">
          <a href="#build_your_cookie_consent">
           Start building your Cookie Consent today.
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="form-group">
         <h2 class="h4 mb-4">
          Do I need Cookie Consent?
         </h2>
         <p class="mb-5">
          The general rule is this: if you use cookies on your website, you need Cookie Consent. For example, if you use Google Analytics, you need Cookie Consent. If you have use JavaScript code on your website that may store or retrieve cookies, you may need Cookie Consent.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="form-group">
         <h2 class="h4 mb-4">
          Is Cookie Consent free?
         </h2>
         <p class="mb-5">
          Yes, Cookie Consent is 100% free.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="form-group">
         <h2 class="h4 mb-4">
          How can I create a Cookie Consent banner notice?
         </h2>
         <p class="mb-5">
          Your Cookie Consent banner notice can be created by going through the builder steps above. At Step 1, choose your consent preference type. At Step 2, customize the banner notice by adding your website name and choosing a preferred language for the Cookie Consent.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="form-group">
         <h2 class="h4 mb-4">
          How can I implement Cookie Consent?
         </h2>
         <p class="mb-5">
          Once you have generated your Cookie Consent banner notice, begin to organize all your JavaScripts by tagging them correctly using the available consent levels: strictly-necessary, functionality, tracking, targeting.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="form-group">
         <h2 class="h4 mb-4">
          How can I edit the text from the Cookie Consent notice banner?
         </h2>
         <p class="mb-5">
          In the current version (3.1.0) the notice banner cannot be modified as we support multiple translations. Modifying the text in English means you'll have to modify the text in 15+ languages. We're working on a new version of the Cookie Consent that would allow you to modify the text. Stay tuned!
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="form-group">
         <h2 class="h4 mb-4">
          Why do I see weird characters in the Cookie Consent notice banner when using different languages?
         </h2>
         <p class="mb-3">
          You may see weird characters in the Cookie Consent notice banner if your HTML page does not have the
          <strong>
           meta charset
          </strong>
          tag added.
         </p>
         <p class="mb-3">
          You can fix this by adding the
          <code>
           &lt;meta charset="utf-8" /&gt;
          </code>
          tag:
         </p>
         <ol class="mb-3">
          <li>
           In the
           <code>
            head
           </code>
           section of your webpages add:
           <code>
            &lt;meta charset="utf-8" /&gt;
           </code>
          </li>
          <li>
           Add the generated Cookie Consent code in the
           <code>
            body
           </code>
           section of your webpages
          </li>
          <li>
           Modify the first line of the generated code to add a new
           <code>
            charset="UTF-8"
           </code>
           parameter:
           <code>
            &lt;script type="text/javascript" charset="UTF-8" src="//.....
           </code>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p class="mb-3">
          <img class="img-fluid" src="/assets/images/cookie-consent-faqs-weird-characters-different-languages.png"/>
         </p>
         <p class="mb-3">
          You should also make sure that your code/text editor saves your webpages in UTF-8 and your web server (Apache, nginx) sets the correct encoding.
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
