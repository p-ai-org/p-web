        <div>
         <strong class="localized" loc-str="result_meaning_title">
          What is FAST.com measuring?
         </strong>
        </div>
        <span class="localized" loc-str="result_meaning_content">
         FAST.com speed test gives you an estimate of your current Internet speed. You will generally be able to get this speed from leading Internet services, which use globally distributed servers.
        </span>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div>
         <strong class="localized" loc-str="why_downloads_only_title">
          Why does FAST.com focus primarily on download speed?
         </strong>
        </div>
        <span class="localized" loc-str="why_downloads_only_content">
         Download speed is most relevant for people who are consuming content on the Internet, and we want FAST.com to be a very simple and fast speed test.
        </span>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div>
         <strong class="localized" loc-str="why_not_ping_and_jitter_title">
          What about ping, latency, upload and other things?
         </strong>
        </div>
        <span class="localized" loc-str="why_not_ping_and_jitter_content">
         When you click the “Show more info” button, you can see your upload speed and connection latency (ping). FAST.com provides two different latency measurements for your Internet connection: “unloaded” and “loaded” with traffic. The difference between these two measurements is also called “bufferbloat”.
        </span>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div>
         <strong class="localized" loc-str="result_calculation_explanation_title">
          How are the results calculated?
         </strong>
        </div>
        <span class="localized" loc-str="result_calculation_explanation_content">
         To calculate your Internet speed, FAST.com performs a series of downloads from and uploads to Netflix servers and calculates the maximum speed your Internet connection can provide. More details are in our
         <a href="https://medium.com/netflix-techblog/building-fast-com-4857fe0f8adb" id="fast-blog-post-link" target="_blank">
          blog post
         </a>
         .
        </span>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div>
         <strong class="localized" loc-str="result_availability_title">
          Will the FAST.com speed test work everywhere in the world?
         </strong>
        </div>
        <span class="localized" loc-str="result_availability_content">
         FAST.com will test Internet speed globally on any device (phone, laptop, or smart TV with browser).
        </span>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div>
         <strong class="localized" loc-str="why_netflix_provides_this_test_title">
          Why is Netflix offering the FAST.com speed test?
         </strong>
        </div>
        <span class="localized" loc-str="why_netflix_provides_this_test_content">
         We want our members to have a simple, quick, ad-free way to estimate the Internet speed that their ISP is providing.
        </span>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div>
         <strong class="localized" loc-str="results_are_low_title">
          What can I do if I'm not getting the speed I pay for?
         </strong>
        </div>
        <span class="localized" loc-str="results_are_low_content">
         If results from FAST.com and other internet speed tests (like
         <a href="https://www.dslreports.com" rel="nofollow">
          dslreports.com
         </a>
         or
         <a href="https://speedtest.net" rel="nofollow">
          speedtest.net
         </a>
         ) often show less speed than you have paid for, you can ask your ISP about the results.
        </span>
       </div>
